The Idea

The design you fall for is the design that gets built.

At this level, the best ideas in a home are usually the first to quietly disappear — trimmed for margin somewhere between the drawing and the drywall. A Distinct Build home doesn't lose them, because the architect and the builder who designed it are the same people who develop and build it. For years they did it for the most demanding architects and clients in the country. Now they do it for themselves.

Architecture: Todd Webb

Nothing gets cut on the way to the front door

The thing that makes a home special is usually the thing under the most pressure — the detail trimmed quietly for margin somewhere between the architect's drawing and the finished house. You feel its absence even when you can't name it.

When the architect and the builder are the ones developing the home, that pressure is gone. No one profits by stripping the design, because the people who own the result are the same people who drew it and built it. What you get is the home an architect and a master builder would make for themselves — finished, and ready to live in.

Each one is distinct. That's the whole idea.

"We synthesize design and construction — eliminating the constant churn of client changes — so we build better homes through a better process."

— Todd Webb

Why It Holds Up

What changes when the architect and builder develop as one.

01

Design integrity, preserved

The vision that makes the house worth a premium is the vision that gets built — end to end, with no one incentivized to cut it.

02

Architect-grade, delivered turnkey

The design you'd otherwise wait two or three years to commission — finished, and available now. The rarest thing in the market.

03

Owner-aligned craft

They own the outcome, so the build isn't sacrificed for a quicker exit. The incentive and the craftsmanship point the same direction.

04

Provenance that stays with the house

Every home carries an architect's name and a builder's record — a story that holds its value and follows the house to its next owner.

05

Proven, present tense

This isn't a theory. The team has built together before, has collaborations underway, and has its first home in construction.
